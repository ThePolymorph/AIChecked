How it works

01

Surface scan

Flags em dashes, literary phrasing, participial chains, buzzwords, uniform rhythm, and essay transitions — in your browser, instantly.

02

Private by design

Text never leaves your device. No account, no API keys, no upload. Good for ad-hoc checks on essays, emails, and drafts.

03

Know the limits

Polished literary AI (Claude, GPT-4) often avoids obvious tells. This is a red-flag pass, not forensic proof. Use your judgment.

Free AI writing checker

AIChecked is a free AI text detector for students, editors, and teachers who need a quick read on whether writing might be machine-generated. Check essays, blog posts, cover letters, and emails for surface patterns common in ChatGPT, Claude, GPT-4, and other large language models — em dashes, rule-of-three lists, AI buzzwords, uniform sentence rhythm, and literary LLM phrasing.

Unlike upload-based AI content checkers, AIChecked runs entirely in your browser. Your text is never sent to a server. Results are signals, not proof — polished AI writing can still score low. Use alongside your own judgment.

AIChecked.com — research tool, not a courtroom.

Inspired by Binoculars (Hans et al., 2024) and DetectGPT (Mitchell et al., 2023). Runs locally with open-source models.
